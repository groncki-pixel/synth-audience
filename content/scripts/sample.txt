FADE IN.

INT. DINER - NIGHT

The fluorescent lights buzz overhead. RAY, late thirties, sits alone at
a corner booth, picking at a plate of fries. The window beside him is
slick with rain. He stares at his phone, then sets it face-down.

The door chimes. MAYA, twenty-something, ducks in shaking water from
her jacket. She catches Ray's eye, hesitates, then walks over.

MAYA
You actually came.

RAY
You said it was important.

She slides into the booth. The waitress drifts past without a word.

MAYA
I need a favor. The kind you don't ask twice.

Ray doesn't answer. He looks at the rain.

CUT TO:

EXT. PARKING LOT - LATER

Ray and Maya stand by his car. Headlights from a passing truck wash
across them and disappear. Maya hands him a small envelope. He doesn't
open it.

MAYA
Promise me you'll think about it.

RAY
I already am.

She nods and walks off into the dark. Ray stays a long moment, then
gets in the car.

FADE OUT.
